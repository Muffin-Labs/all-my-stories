## Desert Hot Springs — The Pariah Moment (Persephone POV)
- On her way to the retreat, she stops at Desert Hot Springs

## The Vipassana Retreat (29 Palms) — Persephone POV

### Arrival & Setting
- Arrives desperate; commits fully despite terror. Total surrender.
- 29 Palms, near Joshua Tree — desert, ancient, indifferent to human stories
- Phone taken. No eye contact. No talking. No journal.
- Schedule: 5:30 AM wake → meditation → meals → meditation → bed → repeat 10 days

### Days 1-4: Anapana (Nose Tip Concentration)
- Focus all attention on the tip of her nose
- **Grasping vs. Gathering:** the harder she grips, the more it scatters
  - Water metaphor: can't grip water. Herding metaphor: push and they scatter.
  - Discovers **gathering attention** — coaxing, corralling with humility
- **The Tarot Connection:** her tarot background becomes a bridge vocabulary
- **Day 4 — Concentration Peace:** genuine peace. The Beaverton Memory — golden light, kid on vacation, nothing to do.

### Days 5-7: Vipassana (Body Scanning)
- Full body scan — she starts to feel "the field"
- **Day 7 — The Deer:** Beaverton memory deepens. Went into the woods. A deer standing in the trees, looking at her. Two beings seeing each other without narrative. **This is the Master.**

### Days 8-10: The Deepening
- **Day 8 — Night Sky:** desert stars. The universe is indifferent to her story. Being nobody is the greatest gift.
- **Day 9 — Ho'oponopono:** reconciliation for everyone she's hurt. Kathleen is the deepest wound. She understands what she took — not just a boyfriend, the play, the shared world.
- **Day 10 — Leaving:** feels she's found something. Unsure if it's real.
